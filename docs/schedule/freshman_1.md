# Details

🎓 Class of 2005: Year 1 (Freshman)
Setting: A trad-leaning public high school in a small town. Atmosphere: Low-rise jeans, pop-punk, the birth of the 24-hour news cycle, and the dial-up screech of Yahoo! Messenger.

🛠️ The Daily "Micro-Loop" (Active Play)
Each day consists of three "Phases" where stats are traded like currency.
The Morning (The Performance):
Class Actions: Pay Attention (Energy-- / Grades++), Pass a Note (Risk++ / Affinity++), or Daydream (Stress-- / Confidence--).
The Slog Killer: Random "Pop Events" (Teacher calls on you) interrupt the routine.
The Afternoon (The Social Market):
Lunch: Choose your table. This is where Perception is built.
Activities: Football practice, Band, or hanging out at the "Diner" (Rebel zone).
The Evening (The Digital Self):
Yahoo! Messenger: Chat with Alex, Chloe, or Sarah. Use "Buzz" to check if someone is actually there.
The "Away Message": Set your status. It influences your Reputation while the game "Auto-Sims" the night.

Focus: Identity labels start to stick. The "Smart but Lazy" struggle begins.
October: The Anthrax Scare / Safety Drills. A month of high Stress. You pass your first risky "Note" to Sarah or Chloe to break the tension.

Focus: Midterms and the "Late Bloomer" seeds.
January: The Midterm Grind. High academic pressure. If you're "Smart but Lazy," this is where you test the Cram Mechanic.
February: The Note-Passing Peak. You and Chloe start passing notes daily. The game tracks how much you reveal to her about your feelings for Sarah.
March: Spring Break. A "Time Skip" where your Weekly Focus determines your stat growth for the whole week without active play.
🔴 Phase 4: Consequences (April – May)
Focus: The "Identity Realization" and the Final Signature.
April: The Mirror Event. Based on your highest Aptitude, the game confronts you with who you’ve become. (e.g., "The Jock who's secretly a Poet").
May: Finals & The End. The "Final Note" delivery. You choose your yearbook signature and set the "flags" for the Sophomore DLC.
🕹️ Unique Gameplay Systems for Year 1
✉️ The "Folded Note" Mechanic
Risk vs. Reward: Writing a note in class is a mini-game. You must stop writing when Ms. Carter looks your way.
The Confidante Multiplier: Notes passed to Chloe are "Safe" and lower Stress. Notes passed to Sarah have a Confidence check; if you fail, you write something "Cringe" that she laughs about with her friends.
🖥️ The Y! Messenger "Status" Game
Your status isn't just flavor—it's a modifier:
"Busy" (Red Icon): Slows relationship decay but prevents new invites.
"Listening to..." (Winamp Integration): If you're listening to the same band as an NPC, you get an Affinity bonus when you next chat.
⛪ The "Trad-Leaning" Flavor (Contextual)
The "Youth Group" Invite: A popular kid (Sarah or Marcus) invites you to a church event.
Accept: Massive Reputation (Popular) boost, but Grit drops.
Decline: You keep your "Cool" (Rebel rep), but Sarah's Affinity takes a hit.

🏁 The Final Graduation (Run End)
The game ends with a "Yearbook Recap" screen:
Stat Summary: Your final GPA, Stress levels, and Top 3 Relationships.
The "Late Bloomer" Flag: A final interaction with Chloe. Does the screen fade to black as she looks at you differently?
The "To Be Continued" Teaser: A glimpse of a Nokia 3310 screen for the Sophomore Year teaser.

📅 Storyboard: Week 1 — "The Food Chain"
Date: August 27 – August 31, 2001 Goal: Define the player's starting "Perception" before the world changes in Week 3.
🌅 Monday: The Threshold
The Scene: Walking toward the front doors. The smell of floor wax and old lockers. Alex (The Connector) runs up to you. He’s wearing a brand-new "Linkin Park" shirt.
The Conflict: Alex is nervous. He wants to promise that you guys will "stick together" no matter what happens this year.
The Decision: 1. "Definitely. We're the dream team." (+Agreeableness, +Alex Affinity). 2. "Let's see how the first week goes first." (+Openness, -Alex Affinity).
The Hook: Alex points out Sarah walking in. You get your first Confidence check. Do you wave? Or do you look at your shoes?
🍎 Tuesday: The Syllabus
The Scene: Ms. Carter’s English Class. The "Smart but Lazy" introduction.
The Conflict: She’s handing out a summer reading diagnostic. You didn't read the book.
The Decision:
Fake it: Use your high Scholastics to guess based on the cover. (Risk/Reward).
The "Slacker" Move: Put your head down. (Increases Rebel Perception, lowers Grades).
The Ripple: After class, Lily (The Overachiever) makes a comment about your score. This establishes her as your academic rival.
🥪 Wednesday: The Lunchroom Siege
The Scene: The first "Real" lunch. The cafeteria is a map of the social web.
The Conflict: Alex is at the "Safe" table (The Loners/Nerds). Sarah is sitting with Marcus and the Populars. There’s an empty chair at the end of their table, but Derek (The Rebel) is also leaning against the wall by the exit.
The Decision (The Social Anchor):
Sit with Alex: (Safety/High Belonging, but 0 Reputation gain).
The "Hover": Try to sit near Sarah. (High Stress, potential Cringe event, but high Reward).
The Outsider: Walk past everyone and go talk to Derek. (Starts the Rebel path).
🖥️ Thursday: The Digital Confidante
The Scene: Late night at home. The dial-up noise. Yahoo! Messenger pings. It's Chloe.
The Conflict: Chloe is stressed. She had a "Public Embarrassment" event in Gym class. She needs to vent.
The Decision (The "Confidante" Lock):
Listen and Support: (Builds Belonging and sets the "Slow Burn" romance flag).
Pivot to Sarah: Tell her you're more worried about what Sarah thought of you today. (Increases Sarah Affinity, but Chloe feels "used").
The Tech Beat: Your first chance to set your "Away Message." This is your first Self-Presentation choice.
🏟️ Friday: The First "Call to Action"
The Scene: The final bell rings. Everyone is talking about the "Back to School" bonfire/hangout.
The Conflict: You don't have an invite. You’re standing by the buses.
The Trigger: Depending on your choices Mon-Thu, a specific character approaches you:
If High Scholastic: Lily asks if you want to join a "Study Group" (Preppy path).
If High Varsity/Rep: Marcus asks if you're coming to the bonfire (Jock path).
If High Rebel: Derek asks if you want to go to the Diner and "actually have fun."
The Weekend Reflection: The game ends the week with a "Memory Layer" screen. It summarizes your new Perception (e.g., "The Quiet One" or "The Wannabe").

📅 Storyboard: Week 2 — "The Fault Lines"
Date: September 3 – September 7, 2001 Goal: Force the player to manage Energy vs. Reputation and introduce the "Group Project" as a long-term stressor.
🌅 Monday: The Weekend Hangover
The Scene: The hallway before first period. The "Status" of the weekend is visible.
The Conflict: If you went to the bonfire/diner in Week 1, you have a "Social High." If you stayed home, you feel out of the loop. Alex is desperate for the "Gossip."
The Decision:
Embellish the story: (+Confidence, +Reputation, but if Sarah/Derek overhears a lie, -Affinity).
Downplay it: (Stabilizes Stress, keeps you "under the radar").
The Vibe: First mention of a "big party" happening later in the month. The carrot is dangled.
🧪 Tuesday: The Group Project (The Anchor)
The Scene: Ms. Carter assigns the first major project (15% of the grade).
The Conflict: You don't get to choose your partner—not yet. She asks for "Volunteers" to lead.
The Decision (The Role Lock):
Volunteer to lead: (+Scholastics, +Grades, ++Stress).
Wait and see: (Alex or Lily will likely be assigned to you).
The Ripple: If you end up with Lily, she starts a "Drill Sergeant" routine. If you end up with Alex, you’re doing all the work while he talks about Yahoo! Messenger.
🥪 Wednesday: The Note Interception
The Scene: Mid-period in a boring History class.
The Conflict: You see a note being passed. It’s from Sarah to her friend, but it gets dropped near your desk.
The Decision (The Integrity Check):
Pass it along unseen: (+Agreeableness, no one knows you did it).
Peek at the note: (You learn a "Secret" about Marcus or the Populars, but if you're caught, massive Cringe and -Reputation).
Pocket it: (Keep the info for a "Rumor" event later).
🖥️ Thursday: The "Buzz" Conflict
The Scene: 11:00 PM. You're "Invisible" on Yahoo! Messenger.
The Conflict: Chloe pings you. She’s crying. Sarah also pings you—she wants the "History notes."
The Decision (The Split Path):
Help Sarah: (Boosts your "Popular" potential, but Chloe sees you are "Active" and feels ignored).
Comfort Chloe: (Deepens the "Confidante" bond, but you miss your window with Sarah).
Try to do both: (Costs double Energy, starts Friday with "Exhaustion").
🏟️ Friday: The Pressure Valve
The Scene: After school. The first "Home Game" (Football).
The Conflict: Marcus (The Athlete) is the hero of the day. There’s a post-game hangout at the park. Derek (The Rebel) approaches you with a "better idea": skipping the game aftermath to go "vandalize" (or just hang out at) the old water tower.
The Decision:
The "Good Kid" Path: Go to the park. (Requires a certain Rep level to not feel like a Loner).
The "Rebel" Path: Go with Derek. (+Grit, +Rebel Affinity, but Ms. Carter or your Parents might find out).
The Weekend Reflection: The game calculates your "Weekly Balance." If you focused too much on Social, your Grades start to dip, triggering the first "Parental Warning" flag for Week 3.

🟢 Week 3: "The Quiet Tuesday"
Theme: The end of the "Before" time. All clique penalties are temporarily suspended.
🌅 Monday (9/10) — The Last Normal Day
School: Mundane drama. You’re trying to find a way to talk to Sarah about a "Mix CD" you made.
Conflict: Alex is obsessed with a new Y!M skin he found. He’s annoying you with "Buzzes."
The Hook: You pass a note to Chloe during History about how boring the teacher is. You’re totally oblivious.
Evening: A normal, long chat on Yahoo! Messenger. Everything feels permanent and safe.
📺 Tuesday (9/11) — The Event
School: The "TV on a Cart" moment. You walk into 2nd Period (Ms. Carter).
The Scene: The room is dark. The TV is on a grainy news channel. No one is talking.
The Conflict: Marcus (Jock) is crying. Derek (Rebel) is staring out the window. Ms. Carter looks like she’s aged ten years.
Mechanical Shift: Your Energy is locked. You can’t "Study" or "Play." Your only action is [Observe] or [Console].
Evening: Dial-up sounds feel like a lifeline. The Y! Messenger list is a sea of "Away" messages. You and Chloe talk until 2 AM.
🏚️ Wednesday (9/12) — The Ghost Town
School: Half the school is missing. The ones who are there aren't in class; they’re in the gym or the courtyard.
The Interaction: You see Sarah sitting alone on a bench.
The Decision: 1. Sit with her: (Massive Affinity gain, suspended clique rules). 2. Give her space: (+Respect, -Confidence).
The Vibe: The "Unity Buff" begins. For the first time, you can talk to anyone without social penalty.
🕯️ Thursday (9/13) — The Gathering
School: Rumors about "what happens next." The school feels smaller, more claustrophobic.
Conflict: A student says something "edgy" or insensitive about the event.
The Decision: Do you [Defend the Unity] or [Stay Silent]? This choice heavily impacts your Agreeableness trait.
Evening: Alex wants to play a game to "forget." Chloe wants to keep talking about the news. You have to choose who to spend your limited Energy on.
🏟️ Friday (9/14) — The Vigil
The Climax: A Friday night candlelight vigil on the football field instead of a game.
The Narrative: You’re standing in a circle. You choose who to stand next to. This choice "Hard-Codes" your primary alliance for the next month.
The Reflection: The screen fades to black with the sound of a lone trumpet. The "Freshman" feeling is officially dead.
🔵 Week 4: "The New Normal"
Theme: The introduction of Risk and the return of the "Social Hierarchy," but with sharper edges.
🇺🇸 Monday (9/17) — Patriotism & Pins

The Conflict: Sarah is heavily involved. She asks if you're joining.
The Decision:
Join in: (+Sarah Affinity, +Popular Rep, but -Rebel Rep).
Watch from afar: (Maintain "Loner" status, +Grit).

School: Security has tightened. Random locker searches are announced.
Conflict: Derek (Rebel) panicked. He has a Zippo and a pack of Marlboro Lights. He tries to slide them into your backpack before the search hits his row.

School: Ms. Carter insists on "returning to normalcy." The Group Project is back on.
The Conflict: You’re paired with Lily (Overachiever). She’s channeling her anxiety into the project and is being a tyrant.

🎭 Week 7: "The Scramble"
Theme: Reaction vs. Action. The "Marcus Proposal" happened, but the ink isn't dry.

The "Saboteur" (Risky): Tell Sarah you heard Marcus only asked her because of a bet. (Massive Risk, +Rebel Rep, Potential Sarah blowback).
The "Confidante" (Deep): Find Chloe and ask what she knows. (She reveals Sarah is actually overwhelmed).

The Scene: Ms. Carter announces that Marcus is on "Academic Probation." If he doesn't pass the Thursday quiz, he can't go to the dance.

Help him: You spend your Tuesday night Y!M time tutoring him. (Sarah sees you as a "Great Guy," Marcus owes you a favor).
