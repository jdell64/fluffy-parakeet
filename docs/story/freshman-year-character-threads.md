# Freshman Year Character Thread Map

## Purpose

This file is the pacing backbone for freshman year social arcs.
It exists to keep scene writing consistent across weeks.

Use it to decide:

- when a character is introduced
- when tension escalates
- when a thread pays off
- which week should feel subtle vs explicit

This is planning structure, not final prose.

---

## Freshman Year Thesis

Freshman year is primarily about friendship drift and identity sorting, not romance resolution.

- **Alex** = drifting longtime friend (never a villain)
- **Sarah** = pedestal crush (high visibility, low emotional access)
- **Chloe** = emotionally accessible friend (grounded, honest lane)
- **Lily** = academic/social friction
- **Derek** = outsider/rebel lane
- **Marcus** = social gatekeeper for the popular lane

Core player question:
Do you keep fighting for old bonds, or accept social change and build new ones?

## Hidden truth (design intent)

Alex is not drifting away *from* the player.
Both the player and Alex are drifting into different versions of themselves.

The player can misinterpret this as loss, rejection, or betrayal depending on their choices.

## Global Writing Rules

- **Access vs Cost:** every social gain should quietly close another door.
- **No perfect lane:** no path is strictly better; each has emotional and systemic tradeoffs.
- **Sarah pressure rule:** Sarah scenes should include at least one pressure source (time, social eyes, interruption, ambiguity).
- **Alex respect rule:** no villain framing, no cheap betrayal, no direct confrontation in early fall.

## Alex Drift Signals (Implementation Guide)

Show drift through small, repeatable signals:

- **Proximity:** not in usual spot, already with different people, exits earlier.
- **Conversation:** shorter replies, new topics, less inside-joke rhythm.
- **Initiative:** player must initiate more often, Alex invites less often.
- **Tone:** still friendly, but less automatic; slight pauses and hesitations.

Do not use early:

- insults
- betrayal beats
- explicit breakup dialogue

## Drift Repetition Rule

Before the player can reasonably accept Alex drift as real, they should experience at least three separate drift signals across different contexts:

- **Physical/proximity signal** (where Alex is, who he is with, when he leaves)
- **Conversational/tone signal** (how Alex talks, what topics feel different, social rhythm changes)
- **Behavioral/initiative signal** (who starts contact, who follows through, invite frequency)

These should occur across multiple weeks before any "undeniable" framing.

## Freshman Year Narrative Spine

### ACT 1 - Arrival (Weeks 1-3)

**Question:** Where do I belong?

- Player starts as a blank slate.
- Alex is the default anchor and easiest social gravity.
- The social map is introduced, but still feels navigable.
- Week 3 remains the emotional pause (temporary unity, hierarchy softening).

**End state:**

- Player has an initial direction.
- Bond with Alex feels intact, or only lightly strained.

### ACT 2 - Differentiation (Weeks 4-8)

**Question:** Who am I becoming?

- Player begins investing in identity lanes and paying early access costs.
- New relationships gain practical and emotional weight.
- Alex alignment becomes less automatic, but not yet "resolved."

**Midpoint Peak (Homecoming):**

- This is a **social climax**, not the year's core story climax.
- Identity visibility spikes, and choices become publicly legible.
- Quiet cost: Alex mismatch becomes noticeable in behavior.

**End state:**

- Player is no longer socially neutral.
- Identity is forming and now interpreted by others.

### ACT 3 - Consequence (Weeks 9-16)

**Question:** What is this costing me?

- Social doors open and close more permanently.
- Academic strain, parental pressure, and burnout begin shaping behavior.
- Alex relationship becomes less frequent, more intentional, and less synchronized.

**Midpoint beat (Midterms):**

- Coping style is exposed under pressure.
- Alex can diverge philosophically (risk-taking vs containment).

**End state:**

- Identity feels "locked in."
- Relationship with Alex is clearly changed from Week 1.

### ACT 4 - Spring Resolution

**Question:** Was it worth it?

- Long-term consequences are acknowledged.
- Social role is stable, but imperfect and constrained by prior choices.
- Alex outcome becomes explicit: maintained, drifted, or fractured.

**Climax/Resolution:**

- Forced Alex + identity decision with no neutral outcome.
- Quiet reflection on social position, relationships, and self-concept.

### Spring Climax - Alex Resolution Arc

**Purpose:** Deliver a true year-level emotional payoff (identity + relationship collision), not just another pressure spike.

**Trigger (April):**

- A high-visibility end-of-year setup where Alex invites the player into his lane.
- The player's established identity pulls in a competing direction.

**Core conflict:**

- Alex and player priorities are incompatible in this moment.
- Both cannot be fully satisfied; the choice has social and emotional cost.

**Player choice (no neutral option):**

- **Choose Alex:** preserve/repair bond at cost to current identity lane.
- **Choose current path:** maintain identity trajectory at cost to the old bond.

**Preferred tone options:**

- Quiet "last normal moment" that lands with recognition, not shouting.
- Values split echoing prior risk-vs-restraint beats.
- Event conflict where lane loyalty and personal history diverge.

**Outcome states:**

- **Maintained** (rare, effort-costly, requires prior investment)
- **Drifted** (default, mutual distance without villain framing)
- **Fractured** (harsh branch from cumulative low trust + oppositional choices)

## Week-by-Week Spine (Weeks 1-11)

### Week 1 - Setup and subtle drift

- Home/family "do not be late" framing sets stakes and tone.
- Alex appears as default friend, but with tiny signs of identity shift.
- Sarah is introduced as pedestal crush (noticed from distance, not emotionally available).
- Chloe/Lily/Derek/Marcus are seeded as lanes, not fully developed.
- **Rule**: no hard breakups, no villain framing, no explicit "we are drifting" speech.

#### Week 1 drift checks

- Player should still believe Alex is their closest friend
- At least 2 subtle drift signals should occur
- No scene should confirm drift explicitly
- Player should be able to rationalize all changes as "normal"

### Week 2 - Routine pressure begins

- Early school-pressure loop (project stress, after-school energy economy).
- Chloe and Alex both present as evening supports, giving player contrast.
- Lily pressure and competence tension begin to bite.
- **Function**: reinforce that social/academic friction is now daily, not event-only.

### Week 3 - Shared crisis, temporary social truce

- 9/11 week flattens cliques and creates temporary emotional unity.
- Sarah connection deepens in quiet, non-romantic framing.
- Chloe and Alex both remain emotionally relevant.
- **Function**: this is not drift resolution; it is an emotional pause that delays conflict.

### Week 4 - Normal returns, sharper than before

- Group boundaries return, but the old rhythm does not fully click back.
- Derek risk choice, Lily project friction, and home dial-up conflict quietly raise pressure.
- **Function**: social sorting resumes with slight misalignment, not hard rupture.

### Week 5 - Realization seed week

- Project outcomes, embarrassment risk, and parent warning pressure converge.
- The player starts suspecting Alex drift is real, but can still rationalize it.
- **Function**: plant recognition without forcing acceptance.

### Week 6 - Identity becomes visible

- The player's social lane is now visible to others.
- Invitations, expectations, and assumptions begin to follow player choices.
- Alex is still present, but no longer aligned by default.
- **Function**: behavioral confirmation of drift through social reality, not speech.

### Week 7 - Social and emotional collision

- Player chooses role in Marcus/Sarah power dynamics.
- High-stakes rumor/tutor/ask choices determine status consequences.
- Alex dynamic reaches a tension point (not necessarily open conflict).
- Player actions now define loyalty-versus-identity direction.
- Chloe route can become stable emotional alternative to pedestal pursuit.
- **Function**: drift becomes emotionally inescapable.

### Week 8 - Event payoff week (dance)

- Spirit Week and Homecoming convert prior choices into visible outcomes.
- Sarah path pays off with tension; Chloe path pays off with relief/intimacy; solo path pays off with mixed autonomy/loneliness.
- **Function**: midpoint peak and identity acceleration, not end-of-year climax.
- Add a post-dance Alex beat that feels subtly "off" (no drama, just mismatch).

### Week 9 - Aftermath and winter lane lock

- Gossip consequences and emotional cooldown.
- Alex is no longer the default hangout; player must choose Alex intentionally.
- Alex/Chloe tension can surface in shared chat conflict.
- Winter activity selection locks medium-term belonging lane.

### Week 10 - Identity maintenance under fatigue

- Grades/parent trust pressure vs social image management.
- Communication with Alex should feel slightly less synced than early weeks.
- Nostalgia beat with Alex can preserve warmth without undoing drift.
- **Function**: old bonds can still matter even as structure changes.

### Week 11 - Burnout and high-risk coping

- Academic pressure spikes, burnout grows, shortcuts tempt.
- Alex midterm-heist beat should read as a philosophical split test (risk vs restraint), not random chaos.
- **Function**: friendship, survival, and ethics begin colliding.

## Character Thread Cards

## Alex - Drifting Longtime Friend

- **Role:** History, familiarity, and the hurt of subtle separation; never framed as evil.
- **Arc target:** Weeks 1-2 mostly normal with micro-friction; Weeks 3-4 less automatic proximity; Week 5 realization seed; Week 6 behavioral confirmation; Week 7 emotional acceptance; Weeks 8-11 branchable outcomes (loyal, distant, strained, situationally allied).
- **Existing signals:** Week 1 "same team?" and diverging lane language; Week 3 normalcy ping (flash game); Week 6 Homecoming check-in; Week 9 Buzz-war with Chloe; Week 10 nostalgia option; Week 11 risky heist ask.
- **Pacing rule:** Do not make Alex a bully or antagonist in freshman fall; later conflict must be contextual and relationship-dependent.
- **Systems:** `alex_affinity`, `alex_drift`, `alex_path`, `player_path`.

### Alex Relationship Arc (Freshman Year)

- **Weeks 1-3:** Strong baseline, shared routine, "we're in this together."
- **Weeks 4-5:** Minor divergence, different interests emerge, still stable.
- **Weeks 6-8 (Homecoming):** Noticeable mismatch, low-drama but present, player may prioritize others.
- **Weeks 9-12:** Reduced automatic proximity, fewer default interactions, communication tone shift.
- **Winter -> Spring:** Relationship becomes conditional and effort-based; can stabilize, drift, or fracture.

### Alex Break Point Requirements (Late Year)

- Must force explicit identity-versus-loyalty decision.
- Must not use betrayal or cartoon antagonism as shortcut drama.
- Must acknowledge prior trajectory (high, medium, or low `alex_proximity`).
- Must lock one of the outcome states before final reflection scenes.

### Alex Drift Curve (System Support)

- Add/track `alex_proximity` (suggested 0-100 scale; starts high).
- Use proximity gates so some scenes only fire when closeness remains high.
- Use low-proximity variants for "same person, different rhythm" interactions.
- Tie decay/growth to initiative choices, hangout selection, and crisis responses.

## Sarah - Pedestal Crush

- **Role:** Aspirational, socially distant crush with brief meaningful moments usually constrained by public context.
- **Arc target:** Weeks 1-3 idealized distance; Weeks 6-8 public pressure around dance politics; Weeks 9+ consequence management rather than romance resolution.
- **Existing signals:** Week 1 quick smile beat; Week 3 quiet bench scene; Weeks 6-8 visibility spike; Week 8 possible high-cost win.
- **Pacing rule:** Keep Sarah meaningful but structurally hard to access; she should trigger projection, nerves, and status pressure more than secure intimacy.
- **Systems:** `sarah_affinity`, `hoco_stance`, `hoco_date`, `hoco_memory`.

---

## Chloe - Emotionally Accessible Friend

- **Role:** The person the player can actually talk to; counterweight to pedestal-chasing and social theater.
- **Arc target:** Weeks 1-2 trust channel setup; Weeks 3-5 consistent emotional availability; Weeks 6-8 possible romantic pivot rooted in friendship; Weeks 9-11 stability test.
- **Existing signals:** Week 2 emotional check-in; Week 3 processing conversation; Week 6 appearance/lane split; Week 7/8 possible date payoff; Week 9 Buzz-war choice.
- **Pacing rule:** Scenes should feel low-drama but high-truth; any romance should read as earned familiarity, not conquest.
- **Systems:** `chloe_affinity`, `chloe_late_bloomer_flag`, optional message-choice tracking.

---

## Lily - Academic/Social Friction

- **Role:** Competence pressure, standards, and resentment triggers; friction, not caricature.
- **Arc target:** Weeks 1-2 classroom tension; Weeks 4-5 project conflict; Weeks 10-11 academic survival versus ego.
- **Existing signals:** Week 1 rivalry seed; Weeks 4-5 process conflict; Week 11 study guide humility test.
- **Pacing rule:** Challenge the player's self-story ("smart but underperforming", "cool but unprepared") while keeping mutual respect possible.
- **Systems:** `grades`, `stress`, optional future `lily_affinity`.

---

## Derek - Outsider/Rebel Lane

- **Role:** Escape from school performance scripts; freedom, risk, and identity by refusal.
- **Arc target:** Weeks 1-3 edge presence; Weeks 4-6 active risk invitations; Weeks 7-11 alternative belonging system (diner/tower/anti-institution choices).
- **Existing signals:** Week 1 lunch lane; Week 4 contraband decision; Week 5 library distraction; Week 8 tower choice; Week 9 diner lane; Week 10 woods party.
- **Pacing rule:** Rebel path should feel liberating now and costly later; avoid reducing Derek to only "bad influence."
- **Systems:** `derek_affinity`, `rep_rebel`, `parent_trust`, `grit`.

---

## Marcus - Popular Gatekeeper

- **Role:** Controls access and norms in high-status social space; can be rival, obstacle, or conditional ally.
- **Arc target:** Weeks 1-3 visible but not central; Weeks 6-8 central in Homecoming power game; Weeks 9+ alliance/sabotage consequences ripple.
- **Existing signals:** Week 1 in Sarah's orbit; Week 3 vulnerability; Week 6 public ask; Week 7 tutor/sabotage branch; Weeks 8-9 aftermath.
- **Pacing rule:** Represent social structure rather than pure cruelty; player choices determine whether Marcus is barrier, partner, or rival.
- **Systems:** `marcus_affinity`, `marcus_sabotaged`, `rep_popular`.

## Week 1 Rewrite Guardrails

- Start at home with family exposition and lateness pressure.
- Keep Alex drift subtle; no hard confrontation.
- Use "counter-pick" social lane logic to hint future divergence.
- Seed all six character roles in light-touch form.
- Ensure Thursday has an actual Chloe emotional scene (not just mention).
- End week with thesis prompt: preserve old bond vs explore new alignment.

---

## Weekly Author Checklist

Use this before finalizing any week:

- At least two character threads advance in a meaningful way.
- At least one choice creates clear **access vs cost**.
- Sarah scenes include pressure and avoid secure-romance tone.
- Alex scenes preserve nuance (no villain coding).
- Emotional tone matches the intended curve for that week.
- Week closes with a forward pull into the next week.

---

## Continuity Guardrails

- Alex is never the villain in freshman fall.
- Friendship drift is gradual and cumulative, not one-scene betrayal.
- Sarah remains mostly pedestal in freshman year pacing.
- Chloe remains the clearest access to emotional honesty.
- Public-status wins should carry private emotional cost.
- Each week should advance at least two character threads, not only one.

## Emotional Curve (Player Experience)

### Weeks 1-2

- Feeling: possibility + slight unease
- Thought: "I could change things this year" -> "this is already harder than expected"
- Social tension begins subtle, then repeats.

### Weeks 3-5

- Feeling: closeness, then discomfort, then suspicion.
- Thought: "maybe this brought us together" -> "things didn't go back to normal" -> "maybe something changed."
- Alex drift moves from ignorable to a seeded realization.

### Weeks 6-8

- Feeling: social performance pressure and emotional mismatch.
- Thought: "people are watching who I am now."
- Identity becomes visible and judged; Alex mismatch becomes noticeable.

### Weeks 9-11

- Feeling: consequence + fatigue
- Thought: "this is who I ended up being"
- Old bonds vs new identity tension solidifies

### Weeks 12-Spring

- Feeling: pressure releases, then emotional reckoning rises.
- Thought: "I survived this year, but what did it change?"
- Story should rise again after Midterms toward the Alex break point, then resolve quietly.
